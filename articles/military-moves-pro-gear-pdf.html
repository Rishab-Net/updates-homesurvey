The Stakes

Pro-Gear Isn’t Just Another Box

On a military move, professional gear — PBP&E: the books, papers, and equipment a service member needs for the job — is weighed separately and doesn’t count against the household-goods weight allowance. Up to 2,000 lbs for the member and 500 lbs for the spouse ride outside the allowance.

If it’s documented, it’s protected weight.

If it’s guessed, it’s an argument.

If it’s missing from the file, it’s the mover’s problem.

Every pound of pro-gear that goes uncounted at survey time turns into a dispute at the scale — and the paperwork burden lands on your ops team, after the truck is already loaded.
